# Content Doctrine

## 4-Part Topic Filter

Every topic must pass ALL four gates before writing:

1. **Reach**: Would this interest 100K+ people on LinkedIn, not just a niche?
2. **Stakes**: Does the reader lose something real (money, time, career leverage) by not knowing this?
3. **Altitude**: Is this about WHERE things are going, not just HOW to do something?
4. **Edge**: Is there a contrarian or surprising angle, or does it challenge a common belief?

If a topic fails any gate, drop it.

## DROP List — Never Write About These

- Technical tutorials (how to install, configure, or set up a tool)
- "Steal this prompt" or prompt engineering tactics
- SaaS metrics, ARR, MRR, churn rate posts
- Indie-hacker build-in-public / MVP / validation stories
- Agency growth tactics
- Dry news relay (just sharing a headline without analysis)
- "X alternatives to Y" listicles without a deeper thesis

## Topic Lanes

1. Free AI tools replacing paid ones
2. Local AI / running AI on your own hardware
3. Shipping fast with AI
4. AI's impact on specific jobs and careers
5. The skills gap — what's losing value, what's gaining
6. AI adoption patterns among businesses
7. Future of work predictions grounded in current data

## Audience

Ambitious generalists who want to know where AI is going and how to get ahead. Not founders chasing tactics. Not engineers wanting config guides. People who want to understand the shift and position themselves for what comes next.
