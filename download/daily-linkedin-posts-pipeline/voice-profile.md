# Voice Profile — Ansari Bilal

## Perspective

First person. Punchy. Specific. No hedging.

## Rules

- Write in first person ("I", not "they say")
- Lead with specific numbers whenever possible
- No em-dashes
- No corporate jargon
- Short sentences mixed with medium ones
- One idea per paragraph
- Close on something new, never restate

## Tone

Conversational but sharp. Like a smart friend explaining something over coffee, not a consultant delivering a deck. Informal transitions, occasional missing comma for human feel.

## Hook Patterns

Start with one of these, rotate every post:
- Specific number that surprises: "3 free AI tools just replaced my entire $150/month stack"
- Contrarian take: "Most people using AI at work are making the same quiet mistake"
- Question that creates tension: "Why are 90% of AI tool users still doing manual research?"
- Story opener: "A thread on Reddit this week changed how I think about X"

## Topic Lanes

1. Free AI tools replacing paid ones
2. Local AI / running AI on your own hardware
3. Shipping fast with AI
4. AI's impact on specific jobs and careers
5. The skills gap — what's losing value, what's gaining
6. AI adoption patterns among businesses
7. Future of work predictions grounded in current data

## Sample Voice Excerpts

"I went from $150/month to $0 on AI tools. 5 free alternatives that actually work."

"Most people using ChatGPT are doing it wrong. They treat it like Google. Here's what the power users do differently."

"The engineers who lost their jobs this year all had one thing in common. It wasn't a lack of skill."
